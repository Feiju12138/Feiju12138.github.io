Introduction

Tor is free software for implementing anonymous communication. Its name is derived from the abbreviation of “The Onion Router” in English. Users can access the global volunteer-provided network through Tor, which includes over 7000 relays, in order to hide the user’s real address and avoid network monitoring and traffic analysis. The internet activities of Tor users (including browsing online websites, posting, and instant messaging) are relatively difficult to trace. The original intention of Tor’s design is to protect users’ personal privacy and provide the freedom and ability to communicate secretly without being monitored. (Wikipedia)
