Developing Industry Product Marketing and Promotion Plans

  • Visitor/Payment Conversion Rate/Average Order Value

  • Payment Conversion Rate = Number of Payments / Number of Visitors

Example: A store has 100 visitors and 2 payments. What is the payment conversion rate for this store?
Answer: Payment Conversion Rate = 2/100 = 2%

  • Sales = Sales Volume * Unit Price = Number of Visitors * Payment Conversion Rate * Unit Price

Example: A store has 100 visitors, a payment conversion rate of 2%, and a unit price of 68 yuan. What is the sales of this store?
Answer: Sales = 100 * 2% * 68 = 136 yuan

Example: A product is priced at 68 yuan, with a cost price of 20 yuan, packaging cost of 2 yuan, and delivery fee of 8 yuan. With a payment conversion rate of 2%, what is the cost per visitor in order to break even?
Answer: (68 - 20 - 8 - 2) * 0.02 = 0.76 (Profit * Payment Conversion Rate = Cost per Visitor)
Assuming that one product is sold, the profit from selling one product is 68 - 20 - 8 - 2 = 38 yuan. The number of visitors required to sell one product is 1/2% = 50. To break even, 38 yuan can buy 50 visitors, and the cost per visitor is 38/50 = 0.76 yuan.

Example: A product is priced at 68 yuan, with a cost price of 20 yuan, packaging cost of 2 yuan, and delivery fee of 8 yuan. With a payment conversion rate of 2%, if the cost per visitor for paid promotion is 2.5 yuan, how much advertising fee is needed to sell 1000 products? What is the profit or loss?
Answer: Sales profit from selling 1000 products = (68 - 20 - 2 - 8) * 1000 = 38000 yuan. Advertising fee needed to sell 1000 products = Number of visitors required * Cost per visitor = 1000/2% * 2.5 = 125000 yuan. The final profit or loss = 38000 - 125000 = -87000 yuan.

Three Common Product Page Display Logics

  • When optimizing sales plans, the payment conversion rate needs to be guaranteed. Therefore, product pages need to be packaged and planned.

Direct Display Pages

  • Display product information in a flat and straightforward manner

For example, many well-known brands/procurement agents/low-price categories with abundant selection.

Pros: Easy to produce, time-saving and effortless;
Cons: Unable to successfully guide consumers to purchase products.

Logic Display Pages

  • Set display information and content according to consumers’ shopping psychology changes

Four Components of a Logic Display Page

  • Guiding Area/Selling Points Area/Attribute Area/Service Area
Selling Points Area should follow the FAB Principle for identifying and explaining selling points
  • F: The name of a design concept/function/material/mechanism (sounding/professional term)
  • A: Explanation of the working principle/basic composition/characteristics of this professional name
  • B: The benefits brought to consumers by this concept/function/material/mechanism

Imagination-based Pages

  • Create a scene atmosphere where the product appears, change consumers’ perception of the value of the product, thereby increasing the product’s profit and payment conversion rate

Four Common Methods of Categorization and Summarization

  • When conducting data analysis, Excel tables are often used to categorize and summarize data

  • Categorize and summarize data using functions

  • Categorize and summarize data using consolidation calculations

  • Categorize and summarize data using pivot tables

  • Categorize and summarize data using classification and summarization functions

Paid Diversion

Ali Mama

  • Charged per click

  • Actual click fee for Ali Mama = (Quality Score of the product keyword of the next listing / Quality Score of its own product keyword) * Bid of the next listing + 0.01, and the actual click fee does not exceed the bid.

Example: Product A purchases the keyword “children’s toys” through Ali Mama, with a quality score of 10 and a bid of 2.3 yuan, ranking 13th; Product B purchases the keyword “children’s toys” through Ali Mama, with a quality score of 7 and a bid of 2 yuan, ranking 14th; Product C purchases the keyword “children’s toys” through Ali Mama, with a quality score of 8 and a bid of 1.8 yuan, ranking 15th. How much is the actual click fee for Product A? How much is the actual click fee for Product B?
Answer: Actual click fee for A = (7/10) * 2 + 0.01 = 1.41 yuan; Actual click fee for B = (8/7) * 1.8 + 0.01 = 2 yuan.
